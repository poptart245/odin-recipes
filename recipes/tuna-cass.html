Tuna Casserole

Description

Tuna casserole is a heartwarming comfort food that reminds me of my childhood. Super quick and easy to make, it is one of my go-to meals. My mom used to make tuna casserole every week when I was growing up, so now every time I eat it, I am instantly transported back to my parents' house.

A pasta-forward dish, tuna casserole is a simple recipe that just requires you to throw all the ingredients together and bake for a short time. Because of this, it is a great weeknight option for a busy family. Paired with a salad and french bread, it was a staple in my family and is sure to be one in yours.

Ingredients

  • One bag large egg noodles, cooked or uncooked
  • Two cans cream of mushroom soup
  • Two cans tuna in oil
  • Two cups sharp cheddar, shredded
  • (Optional) One can peas, drained

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F, placing the rack on the second level.
  2. (Optional)Cook one bag of egg noodles. Drain and set to the side.
  3. Combine the noodles, cans of tuna (not drained), and peas (drained, optional) into a large baking dish.
  4. Add the two cans of cream of mushroom soup along with two can fulls of milk to the mixture and stir to combine
  5. Add one cup of cheese and stir to combine
  6. Place baking dish in oven and bake for 15 minutes
  7. Remove dish from oven. Sprinkle remaining cheese on top and return baking dish to oven. Cook for 15 more minutes or until cheese is golden brown.
  8. Let cool and enjoy
